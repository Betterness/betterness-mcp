# Allergy Season Prep

## Overview
Prepares your immune system for seasonal allergies by analyzing baseline inflammation, immune status, gut health markers, and lifestyle factors weeks before peak pollen season. Allergies are an overreaction of the immune system — this skill identifies what is amplifying that overreaction and helps you build resilience so symptoms are milder or absent.

## MCP Tools Used
- `searchBiomarkers` — Pull hs-CRP, eosinophils, total IgE, vitamin D, ferritin, WBC differential, and histamine-related markers
- `getSleepData` — Assess sleep quality; sleep deprivation increases histamine sensitivity and inflammatory cytokines
- `getSleepStages` — Monitor deep sleep, during which immune regulation and anti-inflammatory cytokine production peak
- `getVitals` — Track HRV as a measure of autonomic balance; low HRV correlates with heightened allergic responses
- `getActivityData` — Evaluate exercise patterns; moderate outdoor exercise can desensitize the immune system, while over-exercise worsens allergies
- `getBodyComposition` — Assess body fat, which produces pro-inflammatory cytokines that amplify allergic responses

## How It Works
1. **Baseline Inflammation Check**: Measures your pre-season inflammatory state. If hs-CRP, eosinophils, or total IgE are already elevated before allergy season, your immune system is primed for a strong allergic response. Lowering baseline inflammation is the most effective allergy prevention strategy.
2. **Eosinophil and IgE Analysis**: Eosinophils above 4% of total WBC and elevated total IgE indicate an immune system already biased toward allergic (Th2) responses. The agent identifies what is driving this bias.
3. **Immune Modulation Assessment**: Checks vitamin D (below 40 ng/mL impairs immune regulation), gut health indicators (most immune regulation happens in the gut), and sleep quality (the immune system calibrates during deep sleep).
4. **HRV and Autonomic Balance**: Low HRV indicates sympathetic dominance, which amplifies mast cell degranulation (histamine release). Improving HRV through sleep and stress management directly reduces allergic reactivity.
5. **Body Composition Factor**: Excess body fat, especially visceral fat, produces IL-6 and TNF-alpha, which prime the immune system for overreaction. The agent quantifies this contribution.
6. **Pre-Season Protocol**: Delivers a 4-8 week preparation protocol timed to begin before your local pollen season starts, including natural antihistamine stacking, immune modulators, and lifestyle adjustments.

## Example Conversation
> **You:** Every spring I am miserable with allergies. It is February now — what can I do to prepare so this year is not as bad?
> **Agent:** Smart timing. Starting preparation 6-8 weeks before peak season gives the best results. Let me assess your current immune baseline.
>
> **Pre-Season Immune Status**:
> | Marker | Your Value | Target for Allergy Resilience | Status |
> |--------|-----------|-------------------------------|--------|
> | hs-CRP | 1.8 mg/L | <1.0 | ELEVATED |
> | Eosinophils | 5.2% | <3% | HIGH (Th2 biased) |
> | Vitamin D | 26 ng/mL | 50-60 | INSUFFICIENT |
> | Total IgE | 185 IU/mL | <100 | ELEVATED |
> | HRV | 38ms | >50 | LOW |
>
> **Assessment**: Your immune system is already in a pro-allergic state. Elevated eosinophils, high IgE, low vitamin D, and elevated baseline inflammation mean when pollen arrives, your immune system will overreact significantly. The good news: every one of these factors is modifiable.
>
> **What is Amplifying Your Allergies**:
> 1. **Vitamin D deficiency**: Below 30 ng/mL, your regulatory T-cells (the ones that tell your immune system to calm down) are underperforming. This is likely your biggest driver.
> 2. **Chronic inflammation**: CRP of 1.8 means your immune system is already on high alert. Sleep data shows 6.1 hours average with only 35 min deep sleep — this is a major contributor.
> 3. **Low HRV (38ms)**: Your autonomic nervous system is sympathetic-dominant, which increases mast cell reactivity.
>
> **6-Week Pre-Season Protocol** (start immediately):
>
> **Weeks 1-6 (daily)**:
> 1. Vitamin D3: 5,000 IU with K2 — most impactful immune modulator for allergies
> 2. Quercetin: 500mg twice daily — natural mast cell stabilizer, takes 2-4 weeks to build effect
> 3. Vitamin C: 1,000mg twice daily — natural antihistamine, lowers histamine levels
> 4. Local bee pollen: Start with 1/4 tsp, build to 1 tbsp — oral desensitization
> 5. Omega-3 (EPA/DHA): 2g daily — shifts immune balance away from Th2/allergic bias
>
> **Lifestyle (start now)**:
> 1. Sleep: Prioritize 7.5+ hours. Your deep sleep must improve to allow immune recalibration
> 2. Nasal saline rinse: Daily — reduces local inflammation and clears allergen exposure
> 3. Moderate outdoor exercise: 30 min daily — builds tolerance to environmental allergens
>
> **Weeks 4-6**: Add stinging nettle extract 600mg daily as a natural H1 blocker as pollen counts begin rising.
>
> **Track Progress**: We will recheck HRV and sleep quality at week 4. If HRV improves above 45 and deep sleep increases to 50+ minutes, your allergic reactivity should be materially reduced.
