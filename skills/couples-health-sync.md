# Couples Health Sync

## Overview
Compares health metrics between two partners who both use Betterness MCP to find shared optimization opportunities, identify how one partner's habits affect the other, and build joint health goals. Covers sleep synchronization, activity matching, biomarker comparisons, and coordinated lifestyle changes that are more effective when done together.

## MCP Tools Used
- `getSleepData` — Compare sleep timing, duration, and quality between partners; mismatched sleep schedules are a major health disruptor for both people
- `getSleepStages` — Analyze how each partner's sleep architecture differs and where shared environment factors (room temperature, noise, light) affect both
- `getVitals` — Compare HRV trends, resting heart rate, and stress patterns to identify shared stressors versus individual ones
- `getActivityData` — Match activity levels, identify exercise compatibility, and find shared movement opportunities
- `searchBiomarkers` — Compare biomarker panels side by side to spot shared environmental exposures or dietary deficiency patterns
- `getBodyComposition` — Track body composition goals together for mutual accountability
- `getBiologicalAge` — Compare biological ages and build a shared longevity strategy

## How It Works
1. **Sleep Sync Analysis**: Compares bedtimes, wake times, and sleep quality between partners. Mismatched schedules (one partner stays up 2+ hours later) disrupt both people's sleep. Identifies if one partner's snoring, movement, or light use is degrading the other's deep sleep.
2. **Shared Environment Impact**: When both partners show similar patterns (both have low vitamin D, both sleep poorly, both have elevated CRP), it often points to shared environmental factors — home lighting, meal patterns, stress sources, or air quality.
3. **Activity Compatibility**: Compares daily steps, exercise frequency, and intensity. Partners who exercise together show 25% better adherence. Identifies activities both can do together based on fitness level differences.
4. **Biomarker Side-by-Side**: Places both partners' bloodwork in a comparison table, highlighting where both need improvement (shared dietary changes), where one can learn from the other, and individual focus areas.
5. **Biological Age Gap**: Compares biological ages and identifies the key factors creating any gap. This reframes health as a shared project rather than individual criticism.
6. **Joint Optimization Plan**: Creates a unified household health protocol with shared goals (meal prep, sleep schedule, weekend activities) and individual focus areas, designed to leverage the accountability of partnership.

## Example Conversation
> **You:** Can you compare my health data with my partner's and find things we should work on together?
> **Agent:** I have pulled both profiles. Here is your Couples Health Sync report.
>
> **SLEEP SYNC**:
> | Metric | You | Partner | Impact |
> |--------|-----|---------|--------|
> | Avg Bedtime | 10:45 PM | 12:30 AM | MISMATCH |
> | Avg Wake Time | 6:30 AM | 7:45 AM | MISMATCH |
> | Sleep Duration | 6.8 hrs | 6.2 hrs | Both under target |
> | Deep Sleep | 48 min | 52 min | Both adequate |
> | Sleep Efficiency | 84% | 79% | Partner lower |
>
> **Key Finding**: Your partner going to bed 1:45 later is likely disrupting your sleep when they come to bed (your sleep efficiency drops to 78% on nights with a >1hr bedtime gap versus 89% when you go to bed within 30 min of each other). Their screen light in the bedroom may be suppressing your melatonin.
>
> **BIOMARKER COMPARISON**:
> | Marker | You | Partner | Both Need |
> |--------|-----|---------|-----------|
> | Vitamin D | 24 ng/mL | 21 ng/mL | YES — shared deficiency |
> | hs-CRP | 1.6 | 2.3 | YES — both elevated |
> | Fasting Glucose | 94 | 102 | Partner: attention needed |
> | Ferritin | 78 | 18 | Partner: iron deficiency |
> | HbA1c | 5.3% | 5.6% | Partner: pre-diabetic range |
>
> **Shared Deficiency Pattern**: You both have low vitamin D and elevated CRP. Since you live together, this likely reflects shared factors: insufficient sun exposure, indoor lifestyle, and possibly inflammatory dietary patterns. Fixing this as a household is far more effective than individually.
>
> **ACTIVITY COMPARISON**:
> | Metric | You | Partner |
> |--------|-----|---------|
> | Daily Steps | 9,200 | 4,800 |
> | Exercise Days/Week | 4 | 1.5 |
> | Avg HR During Exercise | 142 | 128 |
>
> **BIOLOGICAL AGE**:
> - You: Chronological 38, Biological 35 (-3 years)
> - Partner: Chronological 36, Biological 39 (+3 years)
> - Gap driver: Partner's metabolic markers and activity level
>
> **JOINT OPTIMIZATION PLAN**:
>
> **Together (shared habits)**:
> 1. **Align bedtimes**: Agree on a 10:30 PM "screens off, bedroom" rule. This alone could improve BOTH your sleep by 15-20%.
> 2. **Vitamin D**: Both take 4,000 IU D3 + K2 daily with breakfast
> 3. **Weekend walks**: 45-min walk together Saturday and Sunday mornings (boosts partner's steps, gives you both light exposure and vitamin D)
> 4. **Meal prep Sunday**: Mediterranean-style meals for the week to address shared inflammation
>
> **Individual Focus**:
> - **You**: Your health markers are strong. Focus on maintaining consistency.
> - **Partner**: Priority is metabolic health — address fasting glucose and HbA1c through post-meal walks (15 min after dinner together) and iron supplementation for the ferritin deficiency.
>
> **90-Day Shared Goals**:
> - Both vitamin D above 40 ng/mL
> - Both CRP below 1.0
> - Partner: 7,000+ daily steps
> - Shared bedtime within 30 min at least 5 nights/week
